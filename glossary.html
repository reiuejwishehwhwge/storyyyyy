SORT WORDS AND THEIR MEANING

FORTUNE - chance or luck as an external, arbitrary force affecting human affairs. Having lots of money.

LAZY - unwilling to work or use energy

STRAW - an agricultural byproduct consisting of the dry stalks of cereal plants after the grain and chaff have been removed. It makes up about half of the yield by weight of cereal crops such as barley, oats, rice, rye and wheat.

WOODCUTTER - a person who cuts down trees or branches, especially for fuel.

LABORIOUS - (especially of a task, process, or journey) requiring considerable effort and time.

STURDY - (of a person or their body) strongly and solidly built.

FEAR - an unpleasant emotion caused by the belief that someone or something is dangerous, likely to cause pain, or a threat.

ESCAPED - having broken free from confinement or control.

CUNNINGLY - in a clever and deceitful way.

THREATENED - state one's intention to take hostile action against someone in retribution for something done or not done.

HUFF - blow out loudly.

PUFF - a short, explosive burst of breath or wind.

FRUSTRATED - feeling or expressing distress and annoyance, especially because of inability to change or achieve something.

CHIMNEY - a vertical channel or pipe that conducts smoke and combustion gases up from a fire or furnace and typically through the roof of a building.

POT - a container, typically rounded or cylindrical and of ceramic ware or metal, used for storage or cooking.

OVERJOYED - extremely happy.

PRACTICALLY - virtually; almost.

INTELLIGENCE - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills.
